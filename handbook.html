Handbook

Click Beep

Click Beep will begin as an LLC fully owned by William Larson.

Contributors/Employees

Currently, Click Beep does not plan on having hourly or salaried employees. As content is created, contributors will be paid as the business receives money. This will probably be after the YouTube channel crosses 10,000 views, and can be monetized. No payments will be made until after the company has paid for it's startup costs and it's first purchase.

Startup Costs

Legal Zoom filing, Trademark filing, website registration.

First Purchase

Ideally, Click Beep's first purchase will be a computer system capable of 4K video editing, video editing software, a quality camera for video capture.

Other Costs

The company will pay for the following: Cellular Services, Cloud Storage, Email, Gaming services, Internet services, PO Box, and Software.

Equipment

Equipment purchased by the company will be owned by the company. Equipment owned by the company is available, and can be checked out by contributors and employees for personal projects or work related projects.

Videos

Uploading

One of Click Beep's expenses will be internet. So we don't burn through anyone's internet cap, video uploads will be performed on Click Beep's internet connection.

Video Payouts

When the channel hits 10,000 views, videos will begin to generate money. Contributors will earn money based on their role in creating the video, as it earns money. Payments will be monthly, after YouTube commits final numbers for the previous month. Monthly video profits will be split to the contributors in the following manner:

40% - Footage/Performance
Based on the type of video.
Example, game review: If person one captures game footage, and person two acts in from of the camera, screen time being equal, each person receives 20%.
Example, game footage: Person one records five minutes of game footage for a video, they receive all 40%.

25% - Click Beep
The business has to make money, money goes to buy equipment for better videos, items to review, games, tickets to E3, etc.

15% - Editing
Whoever does the majority of video edits.

10% - Idea
Original concept for a video.

5% - Equipment
Whoever owns the majority of the equipment used to create the footage - cameras, console, desktop, set, etc.

5% - Script
If there is no script, this goes to the editor.

= 100%

Videos will not be uploaded unless all parties are in agreement on the breakout of the responsibilities.

Individuals may choose to waive their contributions. Example - Person one creates the concept, scripts the video, creates game footage, uses their equipment to record game footage, person two edits the video and takes credit for video editing, but does not wish to recieve 2.5% of equipment for using their computer to edit the video.

Copyright Claims

If your footage runs afoul of YouTube's copyright protection, that's on you. If the footage needs to be re-edited and re-uploaded to remove copyrighted content, the editor gets 10% out of your cut. If we cannot monetize a video because of this, no one makes any money for that particular video.

Video Removal

We will remove videos to comply with legal requests, and copyright requests. If a contributor wants to remove a video, they will be examined on a case by case basis.

Written Reviews

25% - Click Beep
The business has to make money, money goes to buy equipment for better videos, items to review, games, tickets to E3, etc.

65% - Writer
Whoever writes the article gets this.

10% - Proof/Edit
Final grammar check and proof read.
